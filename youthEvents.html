Grant County

All groups and advocacy are free and confidential. There is a very quick intake process to join each group or advocacy. All Family Advocates, Inc. clients are elligible for each program.

Advocacy

One-on-one advocacy is a time for the youth to meet with a youth advocate, individually. During that time they discuss various topics that the youth, and protective parent, want to talk about. We continue to use art and play therapy techniques through the program A Window Between Worlds. Through this program we are able to find art and play activities to help the youth express the traumas they have been through in order to heal. The protective parent is made aware of progress, changes, and/or concerns about the session while maintaining the youth's right to confidentiality.

Youth Group

Family Advocates, Inc. Youth Group is on Monday nights from 5:00pm-7:00pm We provide a home cooked meal from 5:00pm-5:30pm for all participants After the meal the adults and youth separate for the remainder of the group dependent on volunteers the youth group is split up into three different groups: Infant & Toddlers, Adolescents, and Teens. All groups have an art and embodied art component that gives every youth the opportunity to express themselves. The youth have oppotunities to share what the projects make them feel. The last group of every month is a Together Day where the youth and women have 3 different break out sessions that incorporate play, art, and discussion.

After School Tutoring

Our after school tutoring program is called Kids In Transition (KIT). With transitioning meaning from homelessness to residency. Statistics have proven that youth who become homeless drop between one to two letter grades. Our goal is to have a one-on-one tutoring session with a volunteer and youth with the hope that meeting with tutors twice a week students will maintain and improve their grades. All students are picked up from school and are brought to a single location. There they receive a snack and time to read. After reading they meet with their tutors to complete their homework. If the student completes all of their homework before the end of KIT then they play games with their tutor until everyone has completed their homework.

Summer Day Camp

All Grant County clients will be eligible for the Summer Day Camp. We host a wide array of events from swimming to hiking "The M" to waterballoon games to art activities to drip drip splash. Times and dates vary. For more information call the Youth Services Coordinator at the Grant County Office.

Wrap Around Parents

Wrap Around Parents Mission Statement:

"Wrap Around Parents is a culturally competent, trauma informed support group for clients of Family Advocates, Inc. This group is devoted to educating and empwering families by developing parenting competencies as well as encouraging peer and group led support."

Wrap Around Parents (WAP) meets Wednesdays from 5PM-6:30PM at the Grant County Office. Throughout the group the participants decide what topics will be discussed at each session. There is light refreshments served at the beginning of WAP. Child care is provided for participants of WAP. To enroll in Wrap Around Parents contact Jack Basten at 608-348-5995.

FAMILY ADVOCATES, INC. MISSION

Family Advocates, Inc. is dedicated to the eradication of abuse. Prevention and intervention services will be provided to victims and survivors of domestic, sexual, child, and elder abuse in Grant, Iowa, and Lafayette Counties.

Non-discrimination Statement

Family Advocates, Inc. does not discriminate when providing services on the basis of race, religion, color, national origin, ancestry, age, sex, disability, arrest or conviction record, sexual orientation, sexual identity, marital status, membership in the military reserve, or use or non-use of lawful products.
